Computer Science - Algorithms (Visual and Interactive Explanations)

I noticed that there is a lack of visual and interactive explanations of some slightly more advanced topics in graph theory and algorithms. For this reason, sporadically I make lessons about topics that are difficult to find information on.

Biconnected components are an advanced to intermediate-level graph theory concept. They can be used to solve a lot of different graph problems. Assumed knowledge: what a (directed/undirected) graph is. Notice that there are actually two kinds of biconnected components: vertex-biconnected components, which most online resources talk about, and edge-biconnectedness, which I find easier to explain first (the theory is just a little bit nicer).
